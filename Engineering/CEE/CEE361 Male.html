This class is great. The project is a great way to put all the knowledge together. Virtual instruction is not too good for the kind of material you are taught in this class, but it is manageable. Start the Psets very early and ask lots of questions during office hours. They are doable, but not easy whatsoever.

This has been my favorite course at Princeton so far. The course is very organized and the textbook, lectures, and precepts complement each other well. There definitely is a large time commitment associated with this class though, with weekly problem sets and a fairly detailed structural analysis due at the end of the semester. Still, Professor Bagrianski was very helpful during office hours, so as long as you dedicate enough time to this course, it's definitely worth taking and I highly recommend it to anyone who's interested in structures.
